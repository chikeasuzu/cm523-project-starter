Welcome to Haute History

Haute couture is high-end fashion that is custom-made and designed for individual clients on a case by.
It involves a meticulous process of creating one-of-a-kind, high-quality garments that are made by hand using traditional sewing techniques. Haute couture designs are created by skilled artisans who work in small ateliers, or workshops, and use the finest materials available to create pieces that are unique and of exceptional quality.


Here is a brief history of haute couture fashion using Valentino, Schiaparelli, and Dior as examples

Elsa Schiaparelli was a prominent designer in the 1920s and 1930s who is best known for her innovative use of materials and bold designs. She was one of the first designers to experiment with surrealism in fashion, collaborating with artists like Salvador Dali to create unique, avant-garde designs. Schiaparelli's influence can be seen in many designers today who continue to push the boundaries of traditional fashion.

Christian Dior was a French fashion designer who launched his first collection in 1947, after the end of World War II. Dior's designs were characterized by a feminine silhouette that emphasized the waist and full skirts, which contrasted with the boxy, masculine styles that were popular during the war. Dior's designs were an instant success and helped to reestablish Paris as the fashion capital of the world.

Click here to lean more about Dior's journey with haute couture.

Valentino Garavani, known simply as Valentino, is an Italian designer who rose to fame in the 1960s. His designs were characterized by their use of luxurious fabrics, intricate embroidery, and attention to detail. Valentino's designs were a favorite among high society and celebrities, and he has dressed many famous women, including Jackie Kennedy Onassis and Elizabeth Taylor.

These three designers are just a few examples of the many influential figures in haute couture fashion throughout history. Each designer brought their own unique style and perspective to the industry, and their contributions continue to inspire and influence fashion today.
