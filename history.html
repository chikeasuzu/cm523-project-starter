Haute Couture's first steps can be traced by to Charles Frederick Worth back in 1858. He founded the first true Couture House at number 7, rue de la Paix in Paris.

Haute couture is a term used to describe high-end fashion that is custom-made and designed for individual clients.
It involves a meticulous process of creating one-of-a-kind, high-quality garments that are made by hand using traditional sewing techniques.
Haute couture designs are created by skilled artisans who work in small ateliers using the finest materials available to create unique, quality pieces.

Regular couture, on the other hand, refers to high-end fashion that is made in small quantities, but not necessarily custom-made for individual clients. While regular couture may still be made with high-quality materials and using traditional sewing techniques, the process is less time-consuming and expensive than haute couture. The main difference between haute couture and regular couture is that haute couture designs are made to measure and created specifically for individual clients.

Meanwhile, regular couture is made in small quantities and sold to a broader customer base. Haute couture is typically more exclusive and expensive than regular couture, with prices that can easily reach into the tens of thousands of dollars. Regular couture is still considered luxury fashion, but it is more accessible to a wider range of customers.
